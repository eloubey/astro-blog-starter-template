My family is all on my cousins side for this issue, and I wanted some outside judgement.

My cousin (25m) has always been the golden child of our generation. He’s funny, good looking, and out going, not to mention a boy amongst girls. He’s always loved to mess around and play pranks. For the record, I really dislike this cousin. I think he’s irresponsible, childish, and annoying.

Recently at my grandmothers birthday party we were all eating and drinking on the back porch. My grandmother has a pool. My cousin decided he was going to throw me in the pool because it would be funny to ruin my hair that I just got done that day. I repeatedly told him to stop and put me down, in a very serious and not playful at all tone.

He threw me in the pool, and my hearing aides were ruined. Afterwords when I told him what happened he basically said whoops, didn’t know you had hearing aides now. When I told him the cost of them and that he would have to replace them he freaked out, and said no way.

My family didn’t want me to make him pay for them because i significantly out earn him and also everyone else in the family. He was in college at the time, barely making ends meet in a call center job. His girlfriend and him have a baby and they live in a one bedroom apartment.

He refused to pay, so I took him to court. I won. He refused to make the payments after, so I took him back to court and his wages are now being garnished for the maximum amount, which is 20% of his total wages. It pushed him over the edge of what he could afford, so he’s had to work extra and drop out of college to be able to pay their rent.

My family is absolutely fuming at me. I think he made one bad choice after the other, and he has never given a sincere apology during this whole ordeal. Had he gave a real apology after the incident and asked if he could wait till he finished school to pay me back I would’ve been fine with it. But the entitled little prick is digging his feet in at every move.

AITAH? The only reason I could see me maybe being the asshole is because it’s affecting his child and girlfriend.


🧾 Story Summary

The OP has long-standing hearing issues and recently invested in a $6,000 pair of hearing aids. During a family gathering at their grandmother’s house, their cousin—known for being the “funny prankster” of the family—decided it would be amusing to throw OP into the pool.

Despite clearly and seriously telling him to stop, the cousin ignored the boundary and threw OP in anyway.

The result? The hearing aids were completely destroyed.

When confronted, the cousin brushed it off with a casual “whoops” and refused to pay for the damage. The family backed him up, arguing that OP earns more money and that the cousin couldn’t afford to repay such a large amount due to financial struggles (college, low-paying job, partner, and child).

OP took legal action—and won.

When the cousin still refused to pay, OP escalated it legally again, resulting in wage garnishment (20% of his income). This forced the cousin to drop out of college and work more to survive, sparking outrage from the entire family.

Now OP is questioning: Was pursuing this too far?

🧠 Behavior & Psychology Analysis
1. OP (The Plaintiff)

OP’s behavior is rooted in boundaries and accountability.

Clear communication: They explicitly told the cousin to stop before the incident.
Measured escalation: They didn’t jump straight to court—only after refusal to take responsibility.
Fairness mindset: OP even states they would have accepted delayed payment with a sincere apology.

Psychologically:

Strong sense of justice and personal responsibility
Low tolerance for disrespect and entitlement
Not acting out of revenge, but principle
2. The Cousin (The “Prankster”)

The cousin’s behavior is the core issue.

Boundary violation: Ignoring a clear “stop” is not a prank—it’s disrespect.
Minimization: Calling it “whoops” avoids responsibility.
Entitlement: Refusing to pay despite causing clear damage.

Psychological traits:

Immaturity – inability to foresee consequences
Social reinforcement – likely used to getting away with behavior due to “golden child” status
Avoidance coping – refusing accountability rather than facing consequences

This is a classic case of someone who has never been forced to deal with real consequences… until now.

3. The Family

The family dynamic plays a huge role.

Enabling behavior: They excuse the cousin’s actions due to his financial situation.
Misplaced priorities: They focus on OP’s income instead of the cousin’s responsibility.
Emotional pressure: Attempting to guilt OP by bringing up the cousin’s child and partner.

Psychologically:

Favoritism bias (golden child effect)
Conflict avoidance (easier to pressure OP than confront the cousin)
Collective rationalization (“he didn’t mean it”)
4. Collateral Impact (Child & Girlfriend)

This is the only morally gray area.

The cousin’s poor decisions are now affecting innocent people.
However, this consequence stems from:
The original reckless act
His refusal to take responsibility early
His continued defiance after losing in court

The harm is indirect—but still worth acknowledging.

⚖️ Verdict

NTA (Not The Asshole)

Let’s be very clear:

This was not “just a prank.”
This was destruction of expensive medical equipment after ignoring consent.

OP:

Set boundaries ✔️
Communicated clearly ✔️
Gave opportunity for resolution ✔️
Used legal action only when necessary ✔️

The cousin:

Ignored consent ❌
Caused damage ❌
Refused accountability ❌
Escalated the situation himself ❌
💬 Final Thoughts

This story highlights an important truth:

“Intent doesn’t erase impact.”

It doesn’t matter if the cousin thought it was funny—he caused real harm.

Also:

Being broke doesn’t exempt you from responsibility
Being “funny” doesn’t justify crossing boundaries
Family loyalty should not mean enabling bad behavior

Yes, the outcome is harsh—but it’s the result of repeated bad choices, not one mistake.
